01
Physical Build · Phase One
THE JUDGE'S BENCH
Build the main cardboard podium that holds the laptop and acts as the "courtroom bench." This is your biggest structural piece — do it first.
45
minutes
1
Gather Your Cardboard Boxes Assemble
Find 2 medium-to-large cardboard boxes (think: shoe box, cereal box, delivery box). They don't need to match — you will cover everything later. Stronger/thicker cardboard = more stable bench. Double-walled cardboard is ideal.
  • a
    Box 1 (Bottom): The largest you have. Aim for roughly 40cm wide × 30cm deep × 30cm tall.
  • b
    Box 2 (Top): Slightly smaller or same size. Will sit on top of Box 1.
  • c
    Make sure both boxes are closed and sealed. If open, tape all flaps shut with masking tape first.
TOTAL HEIGHT~70–80cm
WIDTH~50–60cm
DEPTH~30–35cm
MATERIALS: 📦 2× Cardboard Boxes 🖊 Marker pen (for marking cuts)
2
Stack and Bond the Two Boxes Glue Assemble
Place Box 2 on top of Box 1. Center it so there is equal overhang (or no overhang — flush is fine too). This two-box stack becomes the bench body.
  • a
    Apply a thick line of hot glue OR white glue around the entire perimeter of Box 1's top surface.
  • b
    Press Box 2 down firmly. Hold for 30 seconds.
  • c
    Reinforce ALL 4 vertical seams where the boxes meet using 5cm wide masking tape strips. Run the tape vertically across the joint.
  • d
    Test stability: push the top box sideways. If it wobbles, add more tape on the seams. It must feel like one rigid object.
⚠️
Stability is everything. If the bench wobbles and the laptop slides off during the demo, it destroys the illusion instantly. Over-tape is better than under-tape.
MATERIALS: 🔫 Hot glue gun OR white glue 🎨 Masking tape (wide)
3
Cut the Laptop Shelf Notch Cut
The laptop needs to sit on top of the bench at a slight angle (like a real podium) so the screen faces the visitor. Cut a small wedge from a piece of scrap cardboard to act as an angled laptop prop.
  • a
    Cut a rectangle of scrap cardboard: 20cm × 10cm.
  • b
    Fold it at 5cm from one end. This creates an L-shape "foot" that props the laptop back edge up by ~3–4cm, tilting the screen toward the visitor.
  • c
    Place the laptop on top of the bench. Slide the L-wedge under the laptop's back edge. Tape the wedge to the bench top so it doesn't slide.
  • d
    Verify the screen faces a person standing ~70cm away at a natural eye angle. Adjust wedge height if needed.
💡
Ideal screen angle: 10–15° tilt back. The visitor should see the screen clearly without bending down or craning up. Stand at the X mark and verify.
WEDGE HEIGHT3–4 cm
SCREEN TILT10–15°
VISITOR DISTANCE~70 cm
MATERIALS: 📦 Scrap cardboard ✂️ Scissors or box cutter 🎨 Tape
4
Add Internal Bracing (If Wobbly) Assemble
If the structure feels unstable, cut two cardboard strips and insert them diagonally inside Box 1 to create an internal X-brace. This is the trick builders use in large cardboard sculptures.
  • a
    Cut 2 strips of cardboard: each ~40cm × 8cm (matching Box 1's internal diagonal length).
  • b
    Cut a notch halfway through each strip at the center point.
  • c
    Slot the two strips together at the notches to form an X shape.
  • d
    Insert this X brace inside Box 1 (through a flap opening if needed, then re-tape). The bench will feel dramatically more rigid.
🏗️
Engineering note: This X-brace technique is literally the same method used in architectural cardboard modeling and emergency shelter construction. It's surprisingly strong.
MATERIALS: 📦 Scrap cardboard strips ✂️ Box cutter
02
Physical Build · Phase Two
PAINT & FINISH
This phase is the magic trick. Raw brown cardboard looks like a school project. Matte black cardboard looks like a tech product. One coat changes everything.
30
minutes
5
Cover the Bench in Black Paper First Assemble
Before any paint, wrap the entire bench structure in black chart paper or black craft paper. This fills gaps, hides seams, and gives a uniform surface for painting. Think of it like priming a canvas.
  • a
    Cut black paper into large sheets. Wrap each face of the bench like a gift, folding and taping edges to the back.
  • b
    Use a glue stick or double-sided tape to attach sheets flat — no wrinkles or bubbles.
  • c
    Cover all 5 visible sides: front, left, right, back, and top. You don't need the underside.
  • d
    Tape all edges with black electrical tape for a clean, finished border on every edge of the bench.
🛒
Where to get black chart paper: Any stationery shop in Pakistan sells A1 size black chart paper for ~₨30–50 per sheet. You need 3–4 sheets total.
MATERIALS: 📄 Black chart paper (3–4 A1 sheets) 🖊 Glue stick 🔌 Black electrical tape
6
Add Yellow Accent Tape Lines Assemble
Run 2–3 horizontal strips of yellow washi tape or yellow electrical tape across the front face of the bench. These accent lines make it look like a designed product, not just a painted box. Keep them perfectly level.
  • a
    First stripe: 4cm from the top edge of the front face — run yellow tape horizontally end to end.
  • b
    Second stripe: 4cm from the bottom edge of the front face.
  • c
    Optional third stripe: dead center of the front face.
  • d
    Use a ruler to keep lines perfectly horizontal. Crooked tape lines immediately break the illusion.
✨
The yellow is intentional. It matches the yellow accent color of the on-screen verdict text. The physical prop and the software share the same color language. Judges notice this cohesion.
MATERIALS: 💛 Yellow washi/electrical tape 📏 Ruler
7
Add Decorative "Tech Details" Assemble
Stick on a few fake technical-looking details. These are purely decorative but they trigger the brain's "this is a real device" response in viewers.
  • a
    Fake ventilation grille: Cut a 8×4cm rectangle from cardboard. Score horizontal lines 5mm apart with a pen (don't cut through). Glue to the right side of the bench front.
  • b
    Fake status LED: Cut a tiny 1cm circle of red cellophane or red marker on white paper. Stick it near the bottom-right corner. Label underneath in tiny text: "SYS STATUS".
  • c
    Serial number sticker: Write "MC-7742-COURT-UNIT" on a small white label and stick it on the side of the bench. This level of detail makes people look twice.
MATERIALS: 📦 Scrap cardboard 🔴 Red marker / cellophane 🏷️ White sticker labels
03
Physical Build · Phase Three
PROPS & SIGNS
These small props do enormous storytelling work. The gavel alone communicates "courtroom" before anyone reads a word.
30
minutes
8
Build the Cardboard Gavel Cut Glue
The gavel is your single most important prop. It immediately tells visitors this is a courtroom. It takes 10 minutes to make.
  • a
    Head: Cut 3 identical rectangles of thick cardboard: each 8cm × 5cm. Stack and glue all 3 together (making a thick block). Let dry. This is the gavel head.
  • b
    Handle: A pencil, chopstick, or rolled tight cardboard tube ~18cm long.
  • c
    Join: Hot-glue the handle into a notch cut in the center of the gavel head (cut a slot just wide enough for the handle).
  • d
    Finish: Wrap head in brown paper or tan-colored paper. Wrap handle in black electrical tape. Result: a convincing wooden-look gavel.
  • e
    Gavel block: Cut a small square platform (8×8cm, 3 layers thick cardboard glued). Wrap in same brown paper. Place gavel resting on this block on the bench top-left.
HEAD SIZE8 × 5 × 3cm
HANDLE18cm long
BLOCK8 × 8cm square
MATERIALS: 📦 Thick cardboard ✏️ Pencil or chopstick 📄 Brown/tan paper 🔌 Black electrical tape 🔫 Hot glue
9
Make the Front Nameplate Assemble
A nameplate on the front of the bench tells everyone exactly what they're looking at before they even get close. This is the first thing a judge reads.
  • a
    Cut a rectangle of white card or cardboard: 30cm × 8cm.
  • b
    In the LARGEST black marker you have, write: ⚖ MOOD COURT on the top line.
  • c
    Below it in smaller text: POPULATION: YOU
  • d
    Border the card with a thin yellow tape frame around all 4 edges.
  • e
    Glue or tape to the CENTER of the bench front face, between the two yellow accent stripes.
🖨️
Even better: Print the nameplate in a bold font (Bebas Neue or Impact) at large size, cut it out, and glue it. Printed text looks dramatically more professional than handwriting from 2 meters away.
MATERIALS: 📄 White card 🖊 Bold black marker 💛 Yellow tape
10
Make the "STAND HERE" Floor Sign Assemble
The floor X is the most important visitor interaction guide. It defines where to stand so the webcam captures the face correctly. Without it, people stand in wrong spots and the detection fails.
  • a
    The X: Lay two ~40cm strips of bright yellow electrical tape on the floor in an X shape. Place the X exactly 65–70cm in front of the webcam lens. This is the tested detection sweet spot.
  • b
    The sign stake: Tape a white index card to a pencil/chopstick. Write on the card: "STAND ON THE X → YOUR FACE IS EVIDENCE ⚖"
  • c
    Plant the stake in a small ball of paper/tape next to the X so it stands upright on its own, pointing at the X mark.
  • d
    Distance test: Stand on the X yourself, look at the webcam. Run the software. Verify your face is detected within 2 seconds. Adjust X position if not.
🚨
Critical: The X must be at the correct distance for DeepFace to reliably detect emotion. Too far = no detection. Too close = only forehead visible. Test and measure before the exhibition day.
X DISTANCE FROM WEBCAM65–70 cm
X TAPE STRIP LENGTH~40 cm each
MATERIALS: 💛 Yellow electrical tape 📄 White index card ✏️ Pencil/stick 📏 Measuring tape
11
Make the Museum Info Plaque Assemble
A small folded information card in front of the bench — exactly like in a museum. It signals "this is a serious exhibit" to any judge who walks past.
  • a
    Fold an A5 piece of white card in half to make a self-standing tent card.
  • b
    On the front face write or print:
    MOOD COURT v1.0
    Affective Computing · Real-Time Emotion Detection
    Interaction: Stand on X · Await Verdict
  • c
    On the back, list the tech stack in small text: Python · DeepFace · Pygame · OpenCV · No Internet Required.
  • d
    Place this tent card on the bench top-right, next to where the gavel sits.
MATERIALS: 📄 A5 white card 🖊 Marker or printer
04
Software & Hardware · Phase Four
TECH SETUP
Mount the webcam, install dependencies, run the software. The tech side is simpler than the physical build — it's just one USB cable and three pip installs.
45
minutes
12
Mount the Webcam on the Laptop Screen Tech Assemble
The webcam clips to the top edge of the laptop screen or sits on the bench surface aimed at the visitor X mark. This is the only hardware connection in the entire project.
  • a
    If your webcam has a clip: Clip it directly to the top center of the laptop screen. Angle the lens slightly downward (~10°) to capture faces of people standing 70cm away.
  • b
    If your webcam has no clip: Cut a small cardboard bracket that holds it on top of the bench surface, aimed at the X mark at face height. Tape the bracket firmly to the bench top.
  • c
    Cable routing: Run the USB cable down behind the laptop, along the back of the bench, and under the table. Tape every 15cm with black tape so no cable is visible from the front.
  • d
    Plug USB into laptop. Open a webcam test app (or run python -c "import cv2; cap=cv2.VideoCapture(0); print(cap.read())") to verify it works.
📷
If you don't have an external webcam: Use the laptop's built-in webcam. Place the laptop so the built-in camera faces the visitor. The bench design still works — the laptop just sits flat-open on top.
HARDWARE: 📷 USB Webcam (or built-in) 🔌 USB-A cable 🔌 Black tape (cable management)
13
Install Software Dependencies Tech
Open your terminal and run these three commands in order. Do this at home on WiFi — not at the exhibition (DeepFace is a large download ~500MB first time).
  • 1
    pip install deepface — Downloads the facial emotion recognition models (includes TensorFlow). This is the big one. Give it 5–10 minutes.
  • 2
    pip install pygame — The game engine that renders the animated courtroom scene.
  • 3
    pip install opencv-python — Handles webcam capture.
  • 4
    Run the app once at home: python moodcourt.py — First run downloads additional model weights. Subsequent runs start instantly.
🚨
Do NOT skip the first test run at home. DeepFace downloads ~200MB of model weights on first emotion analysis. If you first run at the exhibition on slow WiFi, it will fail or lag badly.
REQUIRES: 🐍 Python 3.8+ 📶 WiFi (for install only) 💾 ~600MB disk space
14
Test All 7 Emotion States Test
Before the exhibition, sit in front of the webcam and verify every emotion triggers the correct courtroom scene. Use exaggerated expressions — the detection works better with big expressions.
  • 😄
    HAPPY: Big genuine smile. Should trigger yellow/gold confetti scene within 1–2 seconds.
  • 😠
    ANGRY: Frown hard, furrow brows. Should trigger red shaking courtroom scene.
  • 😲
    SURPRISED: Raise eyebrows fully, open mouth. Should trigger explosion/chaos scene.
  • 😢
    SAD: Frown downward, look slightly down. Should trigger rainy blue scene.
  • 😨
    FEAR: Wide eyes, slight open mouth. Should trigger purple/dark scene.
  • 😐
    NEUTRAL: Blank face. Should trigger grey "awaiting evidence" scene.
  • 🤢
    DISGUST: Wrinkle nose, squint. Should trigger green murky scene.
⚠️
Lighting matters enormously. Test in the same lighting as the exhibition hall. Dark rooms = poor detection. Bring a small desk lamp to aim at the visitor's face if the hall is dim.
05
Final Assembly · Phase Five
FINAL ASSEMBLY
Bring everything together. Place, position, cable-manage, and do a full dress rehearsal. This phase is about polish — the difference between "looks amateur" and "looks shipped."
30
minutes
15
Set Up the Exhibition Table Layout Final
Position every element relative to each other. The spatial layout is the first thing a judge sees from across the room.
  • a
    Place the bench on the exhibition table. Push it to the back edge so the front face with the nameplate is clearly visible.
  • b
    Place the laptop on the bench with the angled wedge. Open to fullscreen. Screen should face visitors walking past.
  • c
    Gavel + block: top-left of bench. Info plaque tent card: top-right of bench.
  • d
    Floor X: measured 65–70cm from webcam lens. Place the "STAND HERE" stake sign just beside the X.
  • e
    Power cables and charger: routed under the table, completely hidden. No cables should be visible from visitor side.
16
Launch & Lock the Display Tech Final
Set the laptop so it auto-launches into fullscreen mode and cannot accidentally be closed or exit during the demo.
  • a
    Set laptop screen brightness to maximum.
  • b
    Disable screensaver and sleep mode: Settings → Power → Never sleep when plugged in.
  • c
    Run: python moodcourt.py — app launches in fullscreen automatically.
  • d
    Tape a small note to the bench back (not visible to visitors): "Press ESC to quit. Press SPACE = Appeal button."
  • e
    Plug in laptop charger. Tuck charger brick under table. Tape charging cable flat along table leg.
17
Full Dress Rehearsal Test Final
Have one teammate act as the judge and one as the visitor. Run through the complete demo script. Time it. Fix anything that feels slow, confusing, or broken.
  • a
    Visitor walks up cold — no explanation given. Do they figure out to stand on the X? If not, make the floor sign bigger.
  • b
    Visitor smiles. Does the verdict appear within 3 seconds? If slower, check lighting on their face.
  • c
    Press SPACE (appeal). Does a new, funnier verdict appear? Rehearse your verbal reaction to this moment — your laugh sells it.
  • d
    Practice your one-sentence pitch: "It reads your face, decides what emotion you're guilty of, and sentences you accordingly. The appeal always makes it worse."
🎭
The secret of a great demo: Your own reaction to the verdict is part of the show. When the judge gets a ridiculous verdict, laugh with them. Your enthusiasm is contagious. Practice being surprised even when you've seen it 50 times.

🎬 DEMO SCRIPT — SECOND BY SECOND
What happens when the judge walks up. Practice this until it's natural.
0:00sec
FROM ACROSS THE ROOM
Judge sees the bench from 4–5 meters away
They see: a black podium, a glowing screen with cartoon animations, and a yellow X on the floor. The word "MOOD COURT" is readable from 3 meters. Curiosity is triggered before anyone speaks.
0:15sec
JUDGE WALKS OVER
They read the floor sign: "STAND ON THE X → YOUR FACE IS EVIDENCE"
Most people step on the X without being asked. The sign does the work. You say nothing yet — let them discover it.
0:25sec
SYSTEM REACTS
The screen detects their face and emotion — verdict appears
The scene animates. Their emotion label appears. A cartoon judge character reacts. A procedurally generated charge and verdict appear at the bottom.
⚖ CHARGE: Existing too confidently near a camera
VERDICT: Guilty. Obviously. Look at yourself.
0:35sec
YOU EXPLAIN
One sentence: "It reads your face in real-time, detects your emotional state, and the court delivers a legally binding absurd verdict — all running locally, zero internet, zero cost."
Keep it short. The demo is already running. Don't over-explain.
0:50sec
THE APPEAL
Press SPACEBAR. Say: "They can appeal… but the appeal always makes it worse."
New verdict generates. It's more absurd. Everyone laughs. The judge laughs. This is the moment they remember.
⚖ APPEAL DENIED · NEW CHARGE ADDED
Amendment XII: Nobody Asked But Here We Are
1:10sec
TECHNICAL DEPTH
If the judge asks "how does it work?" — explain DeepFace and affective computing
"It uses DeepFace — a library that runs a ResNet model trained on facial action units. It classifies 7 emotions. The scene generator and verdict are procedural — randomized sentence templates so every verdict is unique. The whole thing runs offline on this laptop."
1:30sec
CROWD EFFECT
Other visitors are now watching. They want their turn.
The demo now runs itself. Your job is done. Step back, let others step on the X. A crowd around your booth is the highest signal to passing judges that something interesting is happening.
